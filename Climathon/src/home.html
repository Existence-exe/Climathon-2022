Refuse

This is the overarching first step of the 5R principle, which can help each individual eliminate a major proportion of their waste.

To Refuse - ask yourself “Why am I buying this?” or “Why am I engaging in this activity?”, and if you do not have a need for the item, do not purchase it. You are having a party, and you go to the store to buy a few disposable plates, and see plastic ones. You instinctively go there. But you stop yourself, you find that you don’t really need it. Why? Because there’s paper plates right beside. You choose those.

It is also the step that involves actively changing your habits and avoiding impulses to get everything you like. This is a social problem, and it requires a change in perceptions and awareness. Each step you take and encourage others to take will help push this change forward.

Reduce

The next step is to Reduce. You’ve asked yourself whether you need something and you think you do. The next step is to ask if you need exactly that item.

Right, you are picking paper plates for a party, do you need the 200 piece-pack for your 25 person party? No? Well, then go for the 50 piece pack.

We indulge ourselves with excess, we feel that we will probably use something and so we buy a lot of it. Instead, limit yourself.

Get what you need. As long as there isn’t another pandemic, you should be able to go to the store again in a couple of days.

Reuse

This is one of the more popular Rs! Reusing something is an efficient way to keep something away from the landfill and get good value for money too!

Right then, you see reusable paper plates! Excellent, now you can be the best host for any parties in the future as well. Your party is totally going to be a success!

This one is on the easier side, none of us like to throw away perfectly usable things, and this draws right from that. Let’s maximize the use of our resources!
